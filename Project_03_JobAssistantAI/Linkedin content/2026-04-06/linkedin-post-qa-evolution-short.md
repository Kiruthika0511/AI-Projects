5 years ago, a great QA engineer wrote flawless test scripts.

Today, that same engineer rarely writes code from scratch.

Here is what changed.

The QA role is undergoing a massive transformation. AI agents are now generating hundreds of tests in minutes.

I watched teams stop hand-coding automation loops. Instead, the best engineers are becoming AI supervisors. They engineer the inputs and let the models do the heavy lifting.

But here is the part that stuck with me.

The value of QA hasn't diminished. It has shifted. Without a human strategist guiding the tool, AI just hallucinates bad tests faster.

The new skill stack isn't just automation syntax. It is risk analysis and AI fluency. Your expertise isn't typing repetitive code. It is knowing exactly what the AI missed.

The manual script-writer is obsolete, but the quality strategist is more valuable than ever.
