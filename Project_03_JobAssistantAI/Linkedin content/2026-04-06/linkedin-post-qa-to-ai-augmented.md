I watched a senior QA engineer stop writing test scripts entirely last month.

His team's test coverage didn't drop.

It doubled.

The QA role is evolving faster than most testing teams realize. For years, we measured our worth by the number of scripts we could automate or the manual buttons we could click.

I noticed this shift when reviewing a recent sprint. Instead of writing automation from scratch, this engineer was supervising AI-generated tests. He had become a prompt engineer, crafting smart inputs to guide the AI and letting it generate the coverage.

But here is the part that stuck with me.

His value to the team had completely transformed. By delegating the repetitive script generation, he focused on edge cases, complex business logic, and architectural risk.

The new QA skill stack isn't just coding ability. It is AI tool fluency combined with critical thinking and deep domain expertise. Your value is no longer in clicking buttons or writing boilerplate code. It is in thinking like a quality strategist.

The future of QA isn't writing tests. It is supervising the AI that writes them.
