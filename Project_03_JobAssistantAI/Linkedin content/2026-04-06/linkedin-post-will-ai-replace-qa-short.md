# LinkedIn Post (Short Variant) — "Will AI Replace QA Engineers?"
**Topic:** What AI automates vs. what needs human judgment.
**Style:** Justin Welsh / Content-Production-Prompts.md — Prompt 1

## POST TEXT (Copy below this line)

---

AI caught 400 regressions before my coffee got cold.

It also approved a checkout button that no human user could actually see.

We are automating the wrong part of the job.

I watched a team celebrate reaching 95% automated coverage using AI. Three days later, revenue dropped. The AI scripts verified that the "Buy Now" API sequence worked perfectly. But a CSS layering glitch had hidden the button under a banner.

The AI cared about the code. A human tester would have cared about the experience.

Automation is not a replacement for judgment. It is just a faster way to verify the knowns. The hardest part of QA is discovering the unknowns — the ethical edge cases, the accessibility traps, the moments where the app just feels confusing.

AI will test the paths you tell it to test. You still have to figure out what paths matter.

That is a boundary algorithms will not cross.
