# LinkedIn Post — "Will AI Replace QA Engineers?"
**Topic:** A balanced take — what AI automates vs. what still needs human judgment (exploratory testing, UX, ethics).

## POST TEXT (Copy below this line)

---

AI can write test scripts faster than any engineer on my team.

It cannot tell you when your checkout flow feels wrong to a first-time user.

That distinction is about to matter more than any certification you hold.

I watched a team hand their regression suite to an AI tool. Coverage went from 60% to 90% in three weeks. The manager called it a win. Then a dark pattern slipped into their onboarding screen — one that pushed users toward misleading upsells. The AI passed it. Every run.

Exploratory testing caught it. A human who paused and thought "wait, would I trust this?" caught it.

That is not a gap AI will close anytime soon. Exploratory testing requires forming a hypothesis from feel. UX judgment requires embodied experience. Ethics requires knowing what the product should not do, not just what it can do.

Here is the part that stuck with me: the engineers who documented nothing afterward were the ones most at risk. The engineers who wrote up what the AI missed, why it missed it, and what judgment call they made — those are the ones building irreplaceable skills.

AI will replace the parts of QA that were already automatable. What remains is the work that was always harder to define.

That work is not a consolation prize.
